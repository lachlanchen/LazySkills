# Paragraph And Chapter Gates

## Paragraph Necessity

An accepted paragraph must change at least one item:

- knowledge;
- desire;
- power;
- risk or cost;
- relationship;
- physical position or available path.

Delete or merge a paragraph that only restates atmosphere, theme, surveillance, distress, or lore.

## Independent Review

Score the candidate in context for:

- causal necessity;
- character truth;
- dialogue purpose and subtext when dialogue is present;
- scene movement;
- first-read clarity and natural language;
- non-repetition;
- thematic restraint;
- reader momentum;
- continuity;
- lived sense of place.

The reviewer should identify exact evidence and repair actions. It should not reward decorative language, constant cliffhangers, or philosophical declarations. Absence of dialogue in one paragraph is not itself a flaw.

Use a high threshold, reject any serious continuity error, and cap revision attempts. A blocked paragraph is safer than silent low-quality acceptance.

## Earned Chapter Closure

Word count is advisory. Closure requires:

1. the chapter's immediate desire met, transformed, or decisively denied;
2. the opposing force producing a real cost;
3. the promised irreversible turn occurring on the page;
4. a brief human aftermath;
5. a changed situation that creates a natural future question.

Hardship must follow choice, opponent competence, or plausible systems. Random punishment creates noise, not pressure.
