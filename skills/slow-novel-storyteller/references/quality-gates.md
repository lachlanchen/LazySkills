# Paragraph And Chapter Gates

## Paragraph Necessity

An accepted paragraph must change at least one item:

- knowledge;
- desire;
- power;
- risk or cost;
- relationship;
- physical position or available path.

Delete or merge a paragraph that only restates atmosphere, theme, surveillance, distress, or lore.

## Sentence Necessity

Require every sentence to change at least one item from the paragraph gate: action, choice, knowledge, relationship, power, risk, cost, physical position, or available path. Require descriptive words to affect perception, movement, judgment, or consequence rather than decorate an already complete action.

Use the deletion test: if removing a sentence leaves the character's situation, the reader's understanding, and the next choice unchanged, delete it or merge its necessary fact elsewhere.

Do not confuse efficiency with constant incident. A silence, pause, glance, sound, or landscape detail earns its place when it changes subtext, shortens decision time, blocks a route, reveals cost, or alters what a person can do. Reject:

- atmosphere repeated after the risk is already clear;
- action reports that merely connect two stronger actions;
- two descriptions that perform the same pressure beat;
- dialogue that restates known information;
- functional labels copied from a plan instead of natural references;
- mechanical blocking that records every hand, door, seat, and turn.

Two similar actions may both be necessary when an intervening warning changes their meaning. For example, preparing to open a door and knowingly opening it after hearing the danger are separate choice beats. Two sounds may also remain when the first announces risk and the second forces action. Name the distinct change or remove the repetition.

Apply the gate again in translation. Literal fidelity to locks, gears, handles, routes, or device positions must not turn human decisions into an operating manual.

## Independent Review

Score the candidate in context for:

- causal necessity;
- character truth;
- dialogue purpose and subtext when dialogue is present;
- scene movement;
- first-read clarity and natural language;
- non-repetition;
- thematic restraint;
- reader momentum;
- continuity;
- lived sense of place.

The reviewer should identify exact evidence and repair actions. It should not reward decorative language, constant cliffhangers, or philosophical declarations. Absence of dialogue in one paragraph is not itself a flaw.

Use a high threshold, reject any serious continuity error, and cap revision attempts. A blocked paragraph is safer than silent low-quality acceptance.

Split review into two questions when a project has previously drifted:

1. **Whole-story gate:** Is this still the promised novel, and can the accepted future grow naturally from it?
2. **First-read prose gate:** Is it clear, alive, pleasurable, natural, and inviting before the reader studies it?

Reject polished density when one paragraph tries to deliver too many relatives, images, jokes, symbols, themes, and mysteries. Also reject the opposite failure: orderly summary with correct facts but no particular human voice, friction, appetite, humor, or subtext.

## Blank Space

Leave meaning in gestures, interruptions, omissions, scene cuts, and consequences. Stop explaining after an action already carries the relationship or theme.

Do not confuse blank space with obscurity. A reader may remain uncertain about why something happened or whether a memory is true, but should understand who acted, what was asked, and what physical referent a sentence points to.

Allow paragraphs to disconnect in time, place, or viewpoint when the emotional or causal line remains legible. Do not require ellipses or any other transition device by template.

## Translation Gate

Review accepted-source translation independently. Check kinship, physical sequence, spatial relations, age-appropriate speech, idiomatic domestic actions, repeated words, long modifier stacks, and facts added merely to make a sentence smoother. A faithful translation can still fail if it reads like translated prose.

## Earned Chapter Closure

Word count is advisory. Closure requires:

1. the chapter's immediate desire met, transformed, or decisively denied;
2. the opposing force producing a real cost;
3. the promised irreversible turn occurring on the page;
4. a brief human aftermath;
5. a changed situation that creates a natural future question.

Hardship must follow choice, opponent competence, or plausible systems. Random punishment creates noise, not pressure.
