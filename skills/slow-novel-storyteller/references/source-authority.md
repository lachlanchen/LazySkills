# Source Authority

Use provenance, not file count, to resolve a long project's contradictions.

## Recommended Tiers

1. **Author-originated core:** early drafts, personal essays, notebooks, beatboards, voice samples, and explicit current messages. Preserve desire and worldview even when replacing plot mechanics.
2. **Author-confirmed continuity:** accepted manuscript facts, approved character dossiers, and current volume direction.
3. **Research and reading:** factual texture and abstract craft methods. Verify unstable facts and never copy protected expression.
4. **Generated legacy:** AI drafts, automated outlines, reviews, and logs. Use them to locate possible continuity and known failure patterns, not as stylistic authority.

## Conflict Rules

- Current explicit steering controls unwritten future events but does not automatically erase the author core.
- Accepted prose outranks an outline unless a deliberate rewrite is recorded.
- A character's immediate want and credible options outrank an elegant plan.
- Distinguish historical evidence, folklore, and invention in research notes even when the novel blends them.
- Exclude operational inputs about models, agents, scripts, formatting, and deployment from literary context.

Store the tier and rationale in a human-readable manifest. On a new novelist session, seed from that manifest and the highest-authority raw material instead of a generic synopsis alone.
